# Metrics Definition

This document defines the core product and impact metrics for the Nawah Token ecosystem.
All metrics are derived from the official events listed in `EVENTS.md`.

Metrics are used to measure:
- Product adoption
- User intent and commitment
- Initiative-based impact
- Long-term value creation

---

## Core Metrics (Must-Have)

### Initiative Creation Rate
**Description:**  
Percentage of users who successfully create an initiative.

**Formula:**  
initiative_created / unique_users

**Why it matters:**  
This is the primary indicator of real value creation within the Nawah ecosystem.

---

### Wallet Connection Rate
**Description:**  
Percentage of users who connect a Web3 wallet.

**Formula:**  
wallet_connected / unique_users

**Why it matters:**  
Indicates readiness for decentralized participation and Web3 engagement.

---

### Flow Completion Rate
**Description:**  
Percentage of users who complete a core product flow.

**Formula:**  
flow_completed / flow_started (or unique_users if flow_started is not tracked)

**Why it matters:**  
Measures usability, clarity, and friction within the product experience.

---

## Secondary Metrics

### Initiative Engagement Rate
**Description:**  
Measures how often initiatives receive interaction or support.

**Formula:**  
initiative_supported / initiative_viewed

**Why it matters:**  
Reflects initiative quality and community resonance.

---

### Returning User Rate
**Description:**  
Percentage of users who return after their first interaction.

**Formula:**  
returning_users / total_users

**Why it matters:**  
Signals trust, relevance, and long-term interest.

---

## Guardrail Metrics (Health Indicators)

### Action Failure Rate
**Description:**  
Tracks failures during critical actions.

**Formula:**  
action_failed / (action_success + action_failed)

**Why it matters:**  
Helps detect technical or UX issues early.

---

## Notes
- Metrics must always be derived from approved events.
- Avoid vanity metrics (page views, clicks, impressions).
- Focus on metrics that reflect intent, completion, and impact.
- Metrics should inform decisions, not just reporting.
